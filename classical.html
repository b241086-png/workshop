Classical Music of India

Indian classical music is one of the oldest musical traditions in the world, with roots tracing back over 2000 years. It is deeply spiritual and often regarded as a path to self-realization.

There are two main traditions: Hindustani (North Indian) and Carnatic (South Indian). While both share the same ancient origins, they differ in style, instruments, and performance.

Key elements include the raga (melodic framework) and tala (rhythmic cycles). Traditional instruments include the sitar, sarod, tabla, veena, mridangam, and bansuri.

Great maestros like Ravi Shankar, Bhimsen Joshi, M. S. Subbulakshmi, and Balamuralikrishna have taken Indian classical music to global audiences.
