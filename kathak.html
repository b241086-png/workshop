Kathak

Classical Dance from Northern India

Kathak is a major classical dance form of North India. Known for its intricate footwork, spins, and expressive storytelling, Kathak connects movement with mythology and emotion. Kathak is a classical Indian dance form originating from North India, particularly known for its storytelling aspect and intricate footwork. It evolved from the ancient tradition of Kathakars, who used dance, song, and drama to narrate stories from Hindu epics and mythology.

The word "Kathak" is derived from "Katha" meaning story. Dancers traditionally performed stories from the Ramayana, Mahabharata, and other epics, using hand gestures (mudras), facial expressions, and rhythmic steps. Kathak evolved under the patronage of both Hindu temples and Mughal courts, blending grace with technical mastery.

Performers wear traditional costumes — often a flowing Anarkali dress or a lehenga — and ankle bells (ghungroos) that accentuate every rhythmic move. Today, Kathak is performed on classical stages across the world, preserving the spirit of Indian heritage.
