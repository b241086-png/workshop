Taj Mahal, Agra

The Taj Mahal is a white marble mausoleum built by Mughal emperor Shah Jahan. It is one of the New Seven Wonders of the World.

This UNESCO World Heritage Site is a symbol of eternal love, blending Persian, Islamic, and Indian architectural styles. Its beauty changes with light throughout the day.

Khajuraho Temples, Madhya Pradesh

Khajuraho is known for its stunning temples decorated with intricate erotic sculptures, reflecting India’s artistic excellence.

Built between 950–1050 AD by the Chandela dynasty, these temples represent devotion, passion, and life in stone carvings.

Kathak Dance Performance

One of the eight classical dance forms of India, Kathak narrates stories through rhythmic footwork and expressive gestures.

Originating from North India, Kathak evolved in the Mughal courts and temples, with music and storytelling at its core.

Sun Temple, Konark

This 13th-century temple is shaped like a gigantic chariot and dedicated to the sun god Surya.

It showcases India’s advanced knowledge of astronomy, sculpture, and symbolic architecture. The wheels act as sundials.

Traditional Thali Meal

The Indian thali offers a variety of dishes on a single platter, symbolizing harmony in diversity.

Each region has its unique version — with rice, breads, curries, chutneys, pickles, and desserts served in a balanced way.
