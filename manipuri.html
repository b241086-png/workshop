Manipuri Dance

Grace and Devotion from the Northeast of India

Manipuri is one of the eight classical dance forms of India, originating from the northeastern state of Manipur. It is known for its graceful movements, devotional themes, and traditional costumes.

Unlike many other Indian classical dances, Manipuri focuses on smooth, flowing movements without sharp strikes. It is deeply rooted in the Vaishnavite traditions, often depicting the Raslila – the divine dance of Lord Krishna and Radha. The dance is accompanied by traditional Manipuri music and instruments like the Pung (drum).

Dancers wear elegant costumes — women in cylindrical skirts and translucent veils, men in bright dhotis and turbans. The overall performance radiates serenity and devotion, making Manipuri distinct and spiritually rich.
