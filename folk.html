Folk Music of India

India’s folk music reflects the diversity of its regions, languages, festivals, and rural traditions. Each state and community offers its own unique sound and story.

From the soulful **Baul** songs of Bengal, to the vibrant **Lavani** of Maharashtra, to the desert tunes of **Manganiyars** in Rajasthan — folk music expresses love, nature, daily life, and spiritual devotion.

Instruments like the **ektara**, **dhol**, **shehnai**, and **algoza** bring these stories to life. These traditions are passed orally through generations and are performed at fairs, weddings, and festivals across the country.
