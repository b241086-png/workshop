Dosa is a popular South Indian dish made from fermented rice and urad dal batter. It's a thin, crispy pancake often served with coconut chutney and sambar. A staple in many households and street stalls, dosa comes in many varieties like plain dosa, masala dosa, rava dosa, and more.

The origins of dosa trace back to Tamil Nadu and Karnataka. Traditionally cooked on a cast-iron griddle, it is a healthy and gluten-free food rich in carbohydrates and protein. Masala dosa, filled with spiced potato filling, is one of the most famous variations and a must-try for every food lover visiting South India.
