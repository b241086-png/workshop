Dal Baati Churma is a traditional Rajasthani dish consisting of three components: spicy dal (lentils), baked wheat balls called baati, and sweet churma made from crushed baati mixed with ghee and jaggery or sugar. It is a symbol of Rajasthani hospitality and pride, often served during festivals and feasts.

Baatis are usually baked over firewood or in a clay oven, giving them a unique smoky flavor. The dal is made from a mix of arhar, chana, and moong dals, spiced with Rajasthani tempering. Churma, the sweet element, balances the spicy and savory flavors, making it a wholesome and royal meal.
